The Gentrification Score: A Technocratic Metric for Situated Legitimacy

This paper proposes the Gentrification Score — a composite index for assessing the situated legitimacy of individuals and institutions in speaking or acting on sociocultural and economic matters. Rooted in spatial, institutional, and community belonging, the I.E. score seeks to foreground place-based epistemologies and promote local sovereignty in knowledge production and economic activity. Though the model can be controversial in ethical deployment, it is offered as a conceptual and computational method to recalibrate authority and commerce toward land-bound accountability.
